# Recording Magic

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Magic |
| **Purpose** | Event recording for evidence |

Recording Magic is a specialized branch of sorcery designed to capture and preserve events in a verifiable form. Unlike memory-altering illusions or simple scrying, this magic creates a durable record that can be reviewed later as reliable evidence. The spell functions by encoding sensory data—visual, auditory, and sometimes even magical signatures—into a stable medium, allowing the caster or authorized viewers to replay the captured sequence at will. Its utility in legal and investigative contexts makes it a valued tool among those who seek truth and accountability.

One of the most documented uses of Recording Magic occurred when Arua Raven employed it during a critical interrogation in the Branch Chief's office. By casting the spell before the proceedings began, Raven ensured that every word and action was faithfully preserved, preventing any later distortion or denial of the events that transpired. This application highlights the magic's role in safeguarding against corruption and manipulation, as the recorded evidence can be presented to impartial parties for verification.

Despite its power, Recording Magic is not without limitations. The spell requires a clear intention to record beforehand, and the caster must maintain concentration throughout the duration of the event. Additionally, the magic can be disrupted by certain countermeasures, though such interference itself becomes part of the record if it occurs. As a practice, Recording Magic remains an obscure but respected discipline, often passed down through professional circles such as law enforcement, archives, and select mage orders.

## Usage

Recording Magic is primarily employed to document important events for later reference. In known instances, Arua Raven used it to record the interrogation and subsequent occurrences in the Branch Chief's office, ensuring that the truth of the proceedings was captured and could be verified by others. The spell is cast prior to the event and preserves sensory details without subjective interpretation, making it a reliable evidentiary tool.
