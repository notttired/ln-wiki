# Body Preservation

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Passive Skill |
| **Owner** | Death Fiend |
| **Properties** | |
| Bleeding Stoppage Time | 3 seconds |
| Regeneration Speed | Rapid |
| Effective Counter | Concentrated attacks |

Body Preservation is a passive skill intrinsic to the Death Fiend, granting it an extraordinary regenerative ability that makes it a terrifying opponent on the battlefield. This skill is well-known among monster hunters and adventurers who have faced the creature, as it fundamentally alters the dynamics of combat against the Death Fiend. The skill activates automatically upon injury, initiating a rapid healing process that staunches bleeding and repairs damaged tissue within moments.

Upon sustaining a wound, the Death Fiend's body halts bleeding within three seconds and begins regenerating flesh at an accelerated rate. This rapid recovery renders scattered or non-concentrated attacks nearly useless, as the creature can shrug off injuries that would cripple lesser foes. The skill's efficiency is such that only a focused, sustained assault on a specific area can overcome the regeneration, requiring coordinated efforts from attackers.

A notable instance of Body Preservation's effect was observed in Chapter 23, during an encounter with the renowned warrior Bjorn. Bjorn identified the skill's mechanics and explained its nature to his companions, emphasizing the need for concentrated fire to neutralize the Death Fiend's advantage. This insight proved crucial in formulating a strategy to counter the creature's formidable healing.

## Combat Implications

Due to its rapid regeneration, the Death Fiend requires a strategy of concentrated assault. Isolated attacks are quickly healed, forcing combatants to focus damage on a single area to overwhelm the skill's restorative capabilities. This weakness was famously exploited by Bjorn in Chapter 23, who identified the skill's mechanics and coordinated a sustained attack.
