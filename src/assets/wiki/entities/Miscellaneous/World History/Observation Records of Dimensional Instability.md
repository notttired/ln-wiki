# Observation Records of Dimensional Instability

| | |
|---|---|
| **Title** | Observation Records of Dimensional Instability |
| **Type** | Book |
| **Appears In** | Chapter 19 |

Observation Records of Dimensional Instability is a scholarly treatise that compiles statistics on explorers who have fallen into the Outermost Area. The author, an unnamed researcher, meticulously documents the frequency of such incidents, concluding that occurrences are exceedingly rare. Drawing on these observations, the book puts forth a speculative hypothesis: the precise moment of portal closure may be fundamentally linked to the phenomenon of dimensional instability. This theory, based on patterns observed in portal behavior, offers a potential explanation for the unusual events surrounding the Outermost Area. Bjorn encounters this book during his own investigations, and through it gains critical insight into the rarity of such falls and the author's thought-provoking conjecture.

## Contents

The book begins by presenting raw statistical data on recorded instances of explorers falling into the Outermost Area. It categorizes these events by date, portal type, and other variables, highlighting how uncommon such occurrences truly are. The author then analyzes the data to identify any recurring patterns or anomalies, laying the groundwork for their central speculation.

## Author's Speculation

In the latter portion of the work, the author proposes that dimensional instability—specifically the phenomenon that causes explorers to fall into the Outermost Area—may be triggered when an individual enters a portal exactly at the moment it closes. This theory is derived from correlating the timing of portal closures with known incidents of instability, though the author acknowledges it remains unproven. The speculation serves as the book's most notable intellectual contribution.
