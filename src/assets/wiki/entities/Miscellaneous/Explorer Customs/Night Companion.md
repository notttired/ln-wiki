# Night Companion

| | |
|---|---|

Night Companion is a slang term used among explorers to describe a temporary partnership formed for the purpose of taking turns on watch during the night. The arrangement is born from the necessity of maintaining vigilance in dangerous environments while allowing each participant to rest. While a group of three members is considered optimal for rotating watches, a pair can also function, though it places greater strain on each individual's sleep schedule. The term is widely understood within the explorer community, and such partnerships are commonly dissolved at dawn or when the group reaches a safe haven.

In practice, a Night Companion agreement is informal and based on mutual trust. Participants agree on a schedule for watches, often dividing the night into shifts. The arrangement was notably employed by the protagonist during their journey with a fellow explorer known as Hans. After meeting, the two formed a Night Companion partnership to ensure both could rest without leaving themselves vulnerable to nocturnal threats. This pragmatic collaboration allowed them to travel more safely and efficiently.

The concept reflects the cooperative spirit required for survival in the wilderness, where even strangers must rely on each other. It is a temporary bond, but one that underscores the importance of shared risk and mutual aid.

## Background

Night Companion is a term born from the practical needs of explorers traveling through hazardous regions. With no fixed lodging, individuals must rely on each other to maintain safety during the night. The term itself is a colloquialism, and the practice is widespread among those who traverse dangerous lands. The optimal arrangement involves three members who can rotate watches, allowing each person a full sleep cycle. However, due to circumstances, two-member partnerships are also common, though they require a more taxing schedule of shorter rest periods.

## Usage

The partnership is typically formed upon mutual agreement, with participants deciding on watch shifts and signals. It is understood that each member is responsible for staying alert during their watch and waking the next person. The arrangement was demonstrated when the protagonist formed a Night Companion partnership with Hans during their travel together. Both agreed to take turns keeping watch, allowing each to rest without fear of surprise attacks. This collaboration highlights the ad-hoc nature of such agreements, which are based on necessity and trust, and dissolve once the shared objective is achieved.
