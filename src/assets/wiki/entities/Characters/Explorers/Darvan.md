Darvan is a human explorer who leads a party of four through dangerous territories. He is characterized by his bald appearance and cunning demeanor, often presenting himself as a helpful guide to gain the trust of travelers. His defining trait is his duplicity, as he is willing to feign friendship to achieve his goals. Darvan's actions ultimately reveal a treacherous nature that prioritizes self-interest over loyalty.

During an encounter with Bjorn and Ainar, Darvan offered his assistance, only to betray them at a critical moment. He employed the Suppression ability of a Stone Golem to incapacitate his victims, demonstrating a calculated and opportunistic approach. However, his plan failed when Bjorn mounted a desperate counterattack, striking Darvan in the head with a mace and killing him instantly. This event highlights both Darvan's cunning and his fatal overconfidence.

The circumstances of Darvan's past and the identities of his four companions remain unknown. His legacy is that of a cautionary tale about the dangers of misplaced trust in the wilderness, where allies can quickly become adversaries.

## Background

Little is known about Darvan's origins or early life. He emerges in records as the leader of a group of four explorers, though their purpose and relationship to him are not documented. His motives for offering aid to Bjorn and Ainar before betraying them remain unclear, but his actions suggest a pattern of opportunism.

## Personality

Darvan is treacherous and calculating, willing to feign friendship to lower his victims' guard. He demonstrates patience and strategic thinking, as seen in his use of the Stone Golem's Suppression ability to neutralize threats without immediate confrontation. However, his overconfidence in his plan's success leads to his downfall, as he underestimates his opponents' capacity for resistance.

## Relationships

Darvan's interactions with others are primarily defined by his betrayal. He first approaches Bjorn and Ainar as a friendly ally, offering help before turning on them. The dynamic shifts abruptly when he activates the Stone Golem's Suppression, attempting to subdue them. His death at Bjorn's hands ends the relationship. Darvan also leads a group of four unnamed explorers, but their relationship with him is not recorded.

## Equipment

Darvan possesses a Stone Golem capable of using the Suppression ability, which he employs during his betrayal. The golem's nature—whether it is a construct, a bound creature, or a magical item—is unspecified, but it serves as his primary tool for incapacitating opponents.
