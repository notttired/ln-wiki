Rafdonia is a vast fortress city that serves as the destination for explorers when the Labyrinth closes each cycle. It is a place of stark contrast to the perilous labyrinth, offering a peaceful, modern-style urban environment with tall stone buildings, many exceeding three stories, and running water from faucets. The main road bustles with people, and the city exudes a strong sense of law and order. Crossing the city on foot takes more than an hour, attesting to its size.

The city's society is structured and strict, particularly regarding finances. Tax evasion is considered a grave crime; defaulters are publicly executed by guillotine at the Central Plaza. A peculiar superstition among the locals holds that drinking the blood of such executed tax evaders brings good fortune with money. Non-human residents face a first-year tax of 700,000 Stone, with rates increasing afterward, reflecting the city's complex economic system.

Rafdonia houses essential facilities for explorers, notably the Exchange Office for Ninth-Rank Explorers, where magic stones can be converted into currency. The office is equipped with an emergency button to summon guards, a feature that proved fateful when the explorer Bjorn was arrested there on suspicion. The city's guards are vigilant, maintaining order throughout.

## Purpose

Rafdonia serves as the fortress city to which explorers are transported when the Labyrinth closes each cycle. It acts as a hub for rest, trade, and interaction between expeditions, providing a safe haven from the labyrinth's dangers.

## Appearance

The city is characterized by tall stone buildings, typically three or more stories high, and modern amenities such as running water from faucets. It is vast—traversing the city on foot takes over an hour. The streets are bustling with people, and the overall atmosphere is peaceful and orderly.

## Government and Law

Rafdonia maintains a strong legal system with severe penalties for tax evasion. Defaulters are publicly executed by guillotine at the Central Plaza. The city's guards are ever-vigilant; the Exchange Office for Ninth-Rank Explorers has an emergency button that immediately summons guards, as demonstrated when Bjorn was arrested on suspicion.

## Economy

The city's economy is driven by explorer trade. The Exchange Office for Ninth-Rank Explorers allows magic stones to be exchanged for currency. Non-human residents are subject to a first-year tax of 700,000 Stone, with increasing rates thereafter, reflecting the city's importance as an economic hub.

## Culture

A notable superstition among the populace holds that drinking the blood of executed tax evaders brings good fortune with money. This belief underscores the city's unique blend of harsh justice and cultural quirks.

## Notable Landmarks

Two prominent landmarks define Rafdonia's public life and explorer infrastructure.

### Central Plaza

The central square where public executions of tax evaders are carried out via guillotine. It serves as a gathering place and a stark reminder of the city's strict laws.

### Exchange Office for Ninth-Rank Explorers

A facility where ninth-rank explorers can exchange magic stones for currency. It features an emergency button that summons guards, and it was the site of Bjorn's arrest on suspicion.
