The First Floor is the lowest level of the Labyrinth, serving as the primary entry point for explorers. It is a vast expanse, with Bjorn noting its considerable size during his exploration. The floor is dominated by the Goblin District, a sprawling area inhabited by goblins and other lesser creatures. Despite being the first floor, it is not heavily populated by Barbarians; instead, most adventurers encountered on this level are human, reflecting the floor's role as a training ground or starting zone. The scarcity of Barbarians suggests that they either quickly ascend to higher floors or avoid this area altogether. The First Floor thus stands as a gateway, where explorers begin their perilous journey into the depths of the Labyrinth.

## Geography

The First Floor is known for its considerable size, as noted by explorers. Its main feature is the Goblin District, a large area where goblins are prevalent. The terrain is typical of the Labyrinth's lower levels, with winding corridors and occasional chambers.

## Demographics

The First Floor is primarily inhabited by human explorers who use it as a starting point. Barbarians are rarely seen on this floor, as most either ascend quickly or avoid it entirely. Goblins are the predominant monsters in the area.
