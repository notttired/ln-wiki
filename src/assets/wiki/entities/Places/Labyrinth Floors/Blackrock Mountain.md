Blackrock Mountain is a prominent hunting area located on the Second Floor of the Tower. It is known for its rugged terrain and the diverse array of monsters that inhabit its slopes, including Gnomes, Kobolds, and Stone Golems. These creatures make the mountain a popular destination for aspiring hunters seeking to gather experience and materials. The area's reputation as a challenging but rewarding hunting ground attracts many Adventurers, though its dangers should not be underestimated.

Despite its popularity, Blackrock Mountain has seen fewer visitors since the emergence of Rifts in the Tower. When Hunter Bjorn Yandel was planning his expedition on the Second Floor, he initially intended to hunt at Blackrock Mountain. However, he ultimately decided to forgo the mountain in favor of entering a Rift, lured by the promise of greater rewards and treasures that such unstable dimensional pockets often hold. This decision highlights the shifting priorities of Adventurers in the current era, where Rifts are increasingly seen as more lucrative alternatives to traditional hunting grounds.

## Geography

Blackrock Mountain is a large, rocky peak on the Second Floor. Its slopes are covered in black stone, giving the mountain its name. The terrain is treacherous, with numerous caves, crevices, and rocky outcrops that serve as natural habitats for the monsters that dwell there. The mountain's elevation provides a commanding view of the surrounding landscape, but the harsh environment makes travel difficult without proper preparation.

## Notable Events

In recent times, Blackrock Mountain was the original destination for Hunter Bjorn Yandel's hunting expedition. However, he chose to enter a Rift instead, marking a shift in Adventurer priorities. The mountain continues to be a viable hunting area for those who prefer traditional monster hunting over the unpredictable dangers of Rifts.
