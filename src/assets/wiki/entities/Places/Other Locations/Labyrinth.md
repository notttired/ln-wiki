The Labyrinth is a vast underground dungeon complex, its passages and chambers forming an ant-hill-like structure of interconnected caves. The walls and ceilings are embedded with crystals that emit a steady light, rendering the interior well-lit even in its deeper reaches. The dungeon is accessed via portals, and its floors are traversed sequentially, with the Sixth Floor recognized as a significant milestone for long-term financial security. Time flows differently within the Labyrinth compared to the outside city, creating a temporal disparity that explorers must account for.

The Labyrinth's environment is not uniformly hostile. Open areas, while still dangerous, feel less threatening than confined corridors, though the goblin inhabitants compensate by setting crude traps in these spaces. Water sources in the form of ponds are scattered throughout the dungeon, providing necessary resources for survival. Despite the perils, the Labyrinth offers unique opportunities: the first year after an explorer's entry is marked by reduced taxes and fee exemptions, making it the optimal period for rapid growth and advancement. Explorers like Bjorn set reaching the Sixth Floor as an intermediate goal, leveraging this window for financial and personal gain.

## Appearance and Structure

The Labyrinth consists of caves whose walls and ceiling are embedded with light-emitting crystals, producing a well-lit interior. Its layout is complex, resembling an ant-hill with multiple passages and chambers. Floors are accessed via portals, with the Sixth Floor cited as a notable depth.

## Environmental Features

The Labyrinth contains ponds that serve as water sources. Goblins inhabit the dungeon and set crude traps, particularly in open areas, which pose a hazard despite the reduced sense of threat in those spaces.

## Temporal and Economic Aspects

Time within the Labyrinth passes differently than in the city, creating a temporal disparity. The first year after an explorer's entry grants reduced taxes and fee exemptions, making it the optimal period for rapid growth. This system incentivizes explorers to progress quickly, with milestones such as the Sixth Floor serving as intermediate goals.
