# Raging Horn Bull Inn

| | |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Raging Horn Bull Inn |
| **Aliases** | Raging Horns Inn |
| **Location** | Rafdonia |
| **Daily Rate** | 300 Stone |
| **Sleeping Arrangements** | 10 warriors per room, 5 beds, rotating sleep |

The Raging Horn Bull Inn, also known simply as the Raging Horns Inn, is a low-cost lodging establishment located in the city of Rafdonia. It caters primarily to traveling warriors, with a notable concentration of Barbarians among its guests. The inn is infamous for its cramped and harsh conditions: a single room houses ten warriors, yet provides only five beds, forcing residents to sleep in shifts. The atmosphere is thick with the smell of sweat and hard living, reflecting the rough-and-tumble nature of its clientele.

Ainar, a Barbarian woman, is one of the inn's regular residents. She mentions her stay there when offering assistance to others, and it is at the Raging Horn Bull Inn that Bjorn later finds her. The inn's low price of 300 Stone per day makes it an accessible option for those of limited means, but the shared accommodations and lack of privacy mean only the most hardy or desperate choose to stay there long-term.

Despite its shortcomings, the Raging Horn Bull Inn serves as a gathering point for Barbarians passing through Rafdonia, functioning as both a place to rest and a social hub where warriors exchange news and camaraderie.

## Background

The Raging Horn Bull Inn is situated in Rafdonia, a city that attracts many mercenaries and adventurers. The inn's reputation as a cheap but harsh lodging makes it a common choice for Barbarians who value affordability over comfort. The building itself is unremarkable, but the constant stream of rough patrons gives it a distinct character.

## Pricing and Conditions

The daily rate is 300 Stone, significantly lower than many other inns in the city. However, the cost comes with severe compromises: guests share a room with nine others and must rotate sleep on only five beds. The crowded conditions and lack of ventilation lead to a pervasive odor of sweat. This arrangement is not for the faint of heart, but it allows warriors to stretch their funds further.

## Notable Clientele

Ainar, a Barbarian warrior, resides at the inn. The establishment is frequented by many Barbarians, making it a common meeting point for warriors from various tribes traveling through Rafdonia.
