# Rafdonia Royal Family

| | |
|---|---|
| **Realm** | Rafdonia |
| **Type** | Monarchy |
| **Key Policy** | Execution of Raiders |

The Rafdonia Royal Family is the ruling dynasty of the kingdom of Rafdonia. They hold ultimate authority over the nation's affairs, including matters related to the Labyrinth. However, they have delegated substantial operational control of Labyrinth exploration and management to the Explorer's Guild, allowing the Guild to handle day-to-day activities and lower-level decisions. Despite this delegation, the royal family retains firm control over certain key principles, particularly the treatment of Raiders. The royal family strictly adheres to the policy of executing any individual classified as a Raider, a stance that is non-negotiable and binding on all institutions within Rafdonia.

This uncompromising position on Raiders has practical implications for the Explorer's Guild's operations. As seen in a hostage crisis during the 39th chapter, the Guild's ability to negotiate with hostage-takers is severely limited by the royal decree. The Guild cannot offer amnesty or leniency to Raiders, even in exchange for innocent lives, because the royal family's policy forbids any exception. This creates a difficult position for Guild officials, who must balance their duty to protect citizens with the inflexible laws imposed by the crown.

Overall, the Rafdonia Royal Family is characterized by a pragmatic division of power—allowing specialized bodies like the Explorer's Guild to handle complex Labyrinth affairs—while maintaining absolute authority on matters of justice and punishment. Their rigid adherence to the execution of Raiders reflects a zero-tolerance approach that shapes the legal and operational landscape of the kingdom.

## Governance and Policies

The royal family governs Rafdonia through a centralized monarchy, but they have ceded significant authority over Labyrinth-related activities to the Explorer's Guild. This delegation allows the Guild to manage exploration, monster suppression, and resource extraction without direct royal oversight. However, the royal family retains absolute jurisdiction over criminal classification and punishment, specifically regarding Raiders. The law mandating execution for Raiders is a royal decree that cannot be overridden by the Guild or any other body. This policy was notably referenced during a hostage situation in Chapter 39, where the Guild's hands were tied in negotiations due to the family's inflexible stance.
