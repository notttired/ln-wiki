The Holy Land is a sacred communal facility serving the Barbarian tribe. It functions as a child-rearing center where Barbarian children are raised until their coming of age, after which they are welcomed by their parents with the first return ale. Beyond child-rearing, the Holy Land is a place of learning and spiritual development. Barbarians visit to learn how to read from the elders, and the Tribe Chief performs Spirit Engravings here, a crucial rite for warriors. Additionally, the Holy Land serves as a training ground for those seeking to hone their combat skills. Ainar, for example, trained under an elder for six months, learning a special swordsmanship technique, which made her unavailable for Labyrinth expeditions during that period.

## General Functions

The Holy Land serves multiple essential roles within Barbarian society. It is primarily a communal child-rearing facility where children are raised collectively until they reach the age of maturity, at which point they are greeted by their warrior parents with a ceremonial first return ale. Additionally, Barbarians visit the Holy Land to receive instruction in literacy from the elders, and the Tribe Chief resides there to perform Spirit Engravings, a vital ritual that grants warriors their spiritual marks.

## Training Ground

The Holy Land is also a dedicated training site for Barbarian warriors seeking to advance their skills. Elders at the facility teach specialized techniques to students over extended periods. Notably, Ainar committed to a six-month training regimen under an elder, during which she learned a unique swordsmanship technique, temporarily withdrawing from Labyrinth expeditions to focus on her development.
