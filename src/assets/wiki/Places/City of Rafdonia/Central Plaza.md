# Central Plaza

| | |
|---|---|
| **Location** | Rafdonia |
| **Type** | Plaza |
| **Function** | Crossroads and execution site |
| **Details** | |
| Notable Feature | Permanent guillotine |
| Cultural Practice | Bread dipped in blood of executed tax evaders |

The Central Plaza is a large, open square situated at the heart of Rafdonia, serving as a major crossroads for the city's traffic. It is a paved expanse surrounded by buildings and thoroughfares, a central point where merchants, travelers, and citizens converge. The plaza's most notorious feature is the permanent guillotine erected at its center, a stark instrument of public execution. Executions are held regularly for tax evaders, drawing crowds of onlookers. A macabre local custom has arisen: spectators dip bread in the blood of the condemned, treating the execution as a gruesome spectacle. The plaza thus embodies both the city's civic life and its brutal enforcement of law.

## Cultural Significance

The Central Plaza is the site of public executions by guillotine, primarily of those convicted of tax evasion. The executions are public events, and a notable tradition has developed where spectators dip bread in the blood of the executed, reflecting a deeply ingrained cultural acceptance of harsh punishment and a ritualistic approach to death.

## Notable Appearances

In one recorded instance, the travelers Bjorn and Erwen passed through the plaza and witnessed the ongoing execution of tax evaders, observing the crowd's participation in the blood-dipping ritual.
