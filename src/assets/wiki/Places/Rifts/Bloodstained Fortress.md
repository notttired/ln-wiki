# Bloodstained Fortress

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Rift (Instance Dungeon) |
| **Floor** | First |
| **Chapters** | Three (plus outer checkpoint) |
| **Guardian** | Varies (Death Knight / Vampire Duke Cambormir) |

The Bloodstained Fortress is a Rift—a type of instance dungeon—located on the First Floor of the Tower. It is accessed by offering magic stones at the Monument to the Great Sage. Once entered, the adventurer finds themselves beneath a crimson sky, with a dark fortress visible in the distance. The fortress is surrounded by a moat and can be reached via a drawbridge, forming an outer checkpoint. Beyond lies a series of three distinct zones: the city streets of the outer wall, the underground prison of the inner wall, and the Demon Worship Hall within the lord's castle.

The Bloodstained Fortress teems with undead and demonic creatures. Common monsters include Gargoyle Statues, Dead Men, Skeleton Archers, Ghouls, and Death Fiends. The guardian of the fortress is not fixed; while a Death Knight is the expected guardian, the actual guardian encountered can vary. Notably, when the party led by Bjorn and Ainar entered, they faced the Vampire Duke Cambormir, a Rank 5 Advanced Mutant boss, instead of the anticipated Death Knight.

The fortress was first opened by Bjorn and Ainar as their inaugural Rift exploration. They successfully cleared the outer checkpoint and the city streets, encountering numerous undead and Gargoyle Statues. Later, they confirmed the unpredictable nature of the guardian when Cambormir appeared in place of the Death Knight. The Bloodstained Fortress remains a challenging and varied dungeon for those seeking to conquer the First Floor.

## Layout

The Bloodstained Fortress is divided into four distinct areas that must be cleared sequentially: the outer checkpoint, the city streets, the underground prison, and the Demon Worship Hall.

### Outer Checkpoint

The entrance area featuring a drawbridge and a moat. This is the first obstacle for any party entering the Rift.

### City Streets of the Outer Wall

The second area, consisting of narrow streets filled with undead monsters. Adventurers must navigate through this urban battlefield.

### Underground Prison of the Inner Wall

The third area, a dark prison complex beneath the inner wall. The layout is cramped and hazardous, with ambushes from undead and traps.

### Demon Worship Hall

The final area, located in the lord's castle. This is where the guardian of the fortress resides, and it is the most dangerous part of the dungeon.

## Monsters

The Bloodstained Fortress is inhabited by a variety of undead and demonic creatures, each posing unique threats to intruders.

### Gargoyle Statues

Stone statues that animate when approached, attacking with claws and petrifying breath.

### Dead Men

Slow-moving zombies that rise from the ground, relying on sheer numbers to overwhelm foes.

### Skeleton Archers

Skeletal ranged units that fire arrows from elevated positions, harassing adventurers from afar.

### Ghouls

Fast, feral undead that are drawn to the scent of fresh flesh, attacking with claws and teeth.

### Death Fiends

Powerful undead warriors wielding cursed weapons, capable of inflicting debilitating status effects.

## Guardian

The guardian of the Bloodstained Fortress is not fixed; it can vary each time the Rift is entered.

### Death Knight (Expected)

The standard guardian of the fortress, a heavily armored undead knight wielding a greatsword and necromantic magic.

### Vampire Duke Cambormir

A Rank 5 Advanced Mutant boss who appeared in place of the expected Death Knight. He possesses vampire abilities and is significantly more dangerous.
