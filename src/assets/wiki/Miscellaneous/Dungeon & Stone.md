# Dungeon & Stone

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Game |
| **Appears in** | Chapters 6, 18, 19 |

Dungeon & Stone is a game originating from a world separate from the current reality. It is known for its hardcore gameplay and the prevalence of companion non-player characters. The game features three primary attributes: Physical, Mental, and Supernatural Ability, along with thousands of sub-stats that govern a wide array of character capabilities. Sub-stats such as Flexibility enhance evasion and critical hit chances, Vision extends ranged attack range, Smell influences abilities related to the olfactory sense, and Obsession intensifies the strength of a character's desires. Notably, the game's mechanics have tangible effects beyond the original game environment, as observed when individuals from that world utilize its principles in other contexts.

The name 'Dungeon & Stone' itself carries significant weight. Uttering it is considered a forbidden word, particularly for beings known as Evil Spirits. Bjorn, an individual who hails from that original world, must be cautious not to speak the name aloud, as doing so could expose his true nature as an Evil Spirit. This prohibition is documented in a certain book, indicating that knowledge of the game is dangerous in the current world.

Game mechanics within Dungeon & Stone include a satiety system that is only active within the Labyrinth, and the ability for players to improvise skills on the fly. These features contribute to the game's reputation as particularly demanding and immersive.

## Game Mechanics

Dungeon & Stone employs a three-stat system—Physical, Mental, and Supernatural Ability—supplemented by thousands of sub-stats. The game includes a satiety system that functions only within the Labyrinth, and players can improvise skills. The game is noted for its difficulty and the prevalence of companion NPCs.

## Forbidden Word
