# Corpse Golem

The Corpse Golem is a Rank 7 elite monster that serves as the mid-boss of the Bloodstained Fortress Underground Prison. It is a grotesque amalgamation of hundreds of human corpses—arms, legs, and heads packed together into a massive, writhing pile. Each individual body part moves independently, and hundreds of mouths constantly spew fragmented, agonized phrases such as 'it hurts,' 'kill me,' and 'save me,' all in a female voice. The creature's very presence is a cacophony of torment, making it a horrifying guardian of the prison's depths.

In combat, the Corpse Golem fights with brutal, unholy tactics. It hurls piles of corpses like shot puts, which explode on impact, scattering acidic flesh and blood that can cause severe injuries such as eye damage. It also uses its hundreds of arms to pull adversaries close, overwhelming them with sheer mass. The golem exhibits two distinct phases. Initially, it possesses a protective outer shell of hardened flesh that must be burned away; Raven's Flame Baptism proved effective for this purpose. Once the shell is destroyed, the golem's true form emerges: a faster-moving Bone Golem composed of skeletal remains. Its weak point is a magic core located deep within its ribcage.

The Corpse Golem was encountered by Bjorn and Ainar on the third underground floor of the Bloodstained Fortress. After a fierce battle during which they exploited the creature's vulnerability to fire, they shattered its ribs and exposed the magic core, ultimately defeating the monster. Upon its destruction, the Corpse Golem dropped a Corpse Golem Essence, a valuable material.

## Appearance

The Corpse Golem appears as a massive, ever-shifting mound of human body parts—arms, legs, and heads—all pressed tightly together. Every limb moves with a semblance of life, and countless mouths murmur agonized, fragmented pleas and cries in a female voice. The constant movement and sound create an unsettling and horrifying visage that reflects the suffering of the souls used in its construction.

## Combat Phases

The Corpse Golem has two distinct combat phases, each with unique behaviors and vulnerabilities.

### Outer Shell Phase

In its initial phase, the Corpse Golem is protected by a thick outer shell of hardened flesh and tissue. This shell is highly resistant to physical attacks but can be burned away by fire or similar effects. Raven's Flame Baptism was used to successfully strip this layer, forcing the golem into its second phase.

### Bone Golem Phase

Once the outer shell is removed, the Corpse Golem reveals its true form: a skeletal Bone Golem. In this phase, the creature's movements become significantly faster and more aggressive. Its weak point, a magic core nestled within the ribcage, is now exposed and must be destroyed to defeat the golem.

## Attacks

The Corpse Golem employs several devastating attacks that leverage its composition of corpses and its multitude of limbs.

### Exploding Corpse Throw

The golem hurls bundles of corpses like shot puts. On impact, these bundles explode, scattering acidic flesh and blood that can cause serious damage, including eye injuries.

### Arm Pull

Using its hundreds of arms, the golem reaches out to pull enemies closer, dragging them into its mass where they can be overwhelmed.

### Flesh Explosion

A close-range attack where the golem causes its own flesh to erupt outward in a violent explosion, damaging any nearby foes.

## Weakness

The Corpse Golem's primary weakness is its magic core, located deep within its ribcage. The core is only vulnerable after the outer shell is destroyed, revealing the Bone Golem form. Breaking the ribs to expose the core is essential to defeating the monster. Additionally, fire-based attacks are highly effective against the outer shell in the first phase.
