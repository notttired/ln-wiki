# Behel

| | |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Behel |
| **Type** | Ancestral Spirit |
| **First Appearance** | Chapter 23 |
| **Details** | |
| Culture | Barbarian |
| Invocation | Shouted as a war cry during battle |

Behel is the name of an ancestral spirit revered by the Barbarian people. It is invoked primarily as a battle cry, shouted by warriors as they charge into combat. The cry of 'Behel!' is believed to call upon the spirit's strength and ferocity, channeling the power of ancestors into the living. The word itself has become synonymous with the Barbarian war cry, a primal roar that strikes fear into enemies and rallies allies.

During the battle against the Death Fiend, both Bjorn and Ainar were heard shouting 'Behel—laaaaaaa!!' as they surged forward. This invocation demonstrates the deeply ingrained tradition of calling upon Behel in moments of extreme danger or when seeking the courage to face overwhelming odds. The spirit is not an object of worship in temples, but a living presence on the battlefield, embodied in the roar of warriors.

The cultural significance of Behel extends beyond mere vocalization. It represents the Barbarian ethos of honor through struggle and the belief that the spirits of their ancestors fight alongside them. To shout Behel is to declare oneself part of an unbroken chain of warriors, to assert that one's deeds are worthy of the ancestors' legacy. Though much about the spirit's mythology remains unknown, its role as a battle cry is unmistakable.

## Usage

Behel is shouted as a war cry by Barbarian warriors, most notably during the battle against the Death Fiend. The cry 'Behel—laaaaaaa!!' was heard from Bjorn and Ainar as they charged, symbolizing the invocation of ancestral spirit for strength and courage.
