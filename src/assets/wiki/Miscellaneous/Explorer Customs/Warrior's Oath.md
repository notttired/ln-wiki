# Warrior's Oath

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Oath |
| **Cultural Origin** | Barbarian |
| **Binding Type** | Honor-based |
| **First Appearance** | Chapter 26 |

The Warrior's Oath is a sacred vow traditionally sworn by Barbarians on their Warrior's Honor. Unlike magical bindings such as a Fairy's promise, the oath possesses no supernatural enforcement; its power derives entirely from the cultural weight and personal honor of the swearer. Within Barbarian society, breaking a Warrior's Oath is considered an indelible mark of dishonor, making the pledge generally trusted by others who recognize its significance. In practice, the oath serves as a solemn mutual agreement that binds the participants to a stated course of action, often used in life-or-death situations to establish temporary truces or alliances. Notably, during the Eighth Floor Raid, the Barbarians Bjorn and Ainar swore the Warrior's Oath to the Raider to avoid death, demonstrating the oath's role as a last-resort guarantee of good faith.

## Cultural Significance

The Warrior's Oath is deeply embedded in Barbarian tradition, representing a promise made on one's honor as a warrior. While lacking magical compulsion, the oath is upheld by the severe social consequences of breaking it: a perjurer faces permanent disgrace within their community. This cultural enforcement makes the oath a reliable instrument of trust, even among outsiders who understand its weight. The willingness to swear such an oath is often seen as a sign of sincerity and desperation, as it puts the swearer's entire reputation at stake.
