# Dimensional Instability Phenomenon

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Method |
| **Effect** | Unconditional portal opening experience gain |
| **Requirement** | Security promises or oaths |
| **Practitioners** | Experienced explorers |

The Dimensional Instability Phenomenon is a technique employed by veteran explorers to acquire portal opening experience points without the usual constraints. By deliberately exploiting unstable dimensional rifts, users can unconditionally gain experience that would otherwise require successful portal travel. However, the method demands strict security measures, often enforced through binding promises or oaths, to prevent catastrophic consequences. The phenomenon is known only to seasoned adventurers who understand the delicate balance of dimensional energies. Bjorn, an experienced explorer, once considered utilizing this phenomenon to expedite his progress but ultimately decided to wait for a trustworthy team, highlighting the inherent risks and the need for reliable partners when dealing with such volatile forces. The method remains a controversial but powerful tool in the explorer's arsenal.

## Background

The Dimensional Instability Phenomenon occurs when dimensional barriers weaken, allowing explorers to siphon experience from the chaos. Only those with extensive experience and understanding of dimensional mechanics can attempt it safely. The process is tied to the fundamental instability of certain regions, making it both a resource and a hazard.

## Application

To utilize the phenomenon, an explorer must first secure a binding promise or oath from themselves or others, ensuring that the unstable energy is contained. Once security is established, the explorer can channel the instability to gain portal opening experience unconditionally, bypassing the need for actual portal travel. Despite its efficiency, the method is rarely used due to the dangers involved.
