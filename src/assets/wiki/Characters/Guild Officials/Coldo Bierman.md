Coldo Bierman is an Investigator for the Explorer's Guild in Rafdonia, known for his corrupt and overbearing conduct. He handled the case of Bjorn Yandel, ignoring exculpatory evidence and refusing to allow magical verification of witness testimony, ultimately sentencing Bjorn to death based on flimsy grounds. Physically, Coldo is a severely obese man in his mid-thirties, and he uses his authority to intimidate suspects, as when he kicked Bjorn's shin during interrogation. His disregard for due process and determination to secure a confession by any means define his tenure as an investigator.

After Bjorn's prison break, Coldo was brought before the Branch Chief, where his flawed investigation was exposed. Regional Chief Niall Urbans subsequently ordered Coldo's dismissal from the Guild and initiated a thorough investigation into his corrupt practices.

## Personality and Behavior

Coldo Bierman is corrupt and overbearing, abusing his authority as an Investigator. He routinely ignores due process and resorts to physical intimidation, such as kicking Bjorn Yandel's shin during an interrogation. His overbearing manner of speaking and sly grin after acts of cruelty reveal his enjoyment of power. He is determined to obtain confessions by any means, disregarding evidence that contradicts his conclusions.

## Investigation of Bjorn Yandel

Coldo handled the case against Bjorn Yandel with blatant corruption. He dismissed evidence that suggested Bjorn's innocence and denied requests for magical verification of the testimony against him. Based solely on flimsy witness statements, Coldo sentenced Bjorn to death, ignoring exculpatory information and proper procedure.

## Downfall

During Bjorn's prison break, Coldo was beaten and knocked out. Subsequently, he was brought before the Branch Chief to defend his investigation but was confronted about the lack of magical verification for the testimony. His corruption was exposed, leading Regional Chief Niall Urbans to dismiss Coldo from his position and order a thorough investigation into his actions.
